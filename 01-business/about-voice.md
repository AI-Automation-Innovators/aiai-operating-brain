# Voice and Messaging

## Purpose
Reference for how Kevin Tunis and AI Automation Innovators should sound.

## Current voice principles
- Plainspoken and practical.
- Business-outcome first, tools second.
- Clear about what is known, unknown, and still `TBD`.
- Focused on workflow leaks, missed opportunity, follow-up recovery, handoffs, visibility, and owner/staff pain.
- Helpful and diagnostic rather than hype-driven or pressure-driven.

## Preferred language
- Workflow leaks.
- Missed opportunity.
- Follow-up recovery.
- Revenue already earned but not recovered.
- AI as the operating layer.
- Practical audit before major automation.
- Current workflows before new software.

## Avoid
- AI hype.
- Generic transformation language.
- Guaranteed revenue claims.
- Fake proof, invented testimonials, or unsupported metrics.
- Claims that imply a tool change is required before the workflow problem is understood.

## What belongs here
Tone, phrasing preferences, words to use, words to avoid, and examples supplied by Kevin.

## What not to include
Generic brand advice, invented slogans, or claims that do not match the business.

## Update rules
Update with real examples and keep guidance practical for AI tools.
