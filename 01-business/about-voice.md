# Voice and Messaging

## Purpose

Reference for how Kevin Tunis and AI Automation Innovators should sound.

## Core voice

The voice is plainspoken, practical, and business-outcome first.

It should sound like an operator helping a business owner understand where work is leaking, not like an agency selling hype or a tool vendor pushing software.

## Voice principles

- Lead with the workflow problem before naming tools.
- Keep AI second to the business outcome.
- Be specific about missed follow-up, handoffs, intake, visibility, ownership, and manual bottlenecks.
- Use short sections, bullets, and direct explanations.
- Avoid fake urgency, inflated claims, and vague transformation language.
- Name unknowns as `TBD` instead of filling gaps.

## Preferred language

- Workflow leaks.
- Missed opportunity.
- Follow-up gaps.
- Handoff gaps.
- Weak intake.
- Manual bottlenecks.
- Visibility gaps.
- AI as an operating layer.
- Practical audit before major automation.
- Existing opportunities that are delayed, dropped, or hidden.

## Phrases to avoid

- AI will transform your business.
- Guaranteed revenue recovery.
- You are leaving millions on the table.
- Fully automated growth machine.
- Passive income from AI.
- Revolutionary AI system.
- Proven results, unless proof is documented.
- Client names, metrics, or testimonials that are not confirmed.

## Strong phrasing examples

- "The audit starts with the workflow, not the tool."
- "More leads will not fix follow-up that already gets dropped."
- "AI should help the business see what is stuck, who owns it, and what happens next."
- "This is an audit, not a guaranteed revenue claim."
- "Pricing, timeline, and final deliverables are `TBD` until Kevin confirms them."

## Weak or hype-heavy phrasing examples

- "We use cutting-edge AI to revolutionize your operations."
- "This system guarantees recovered revenue."
- "Our proven method adds millions in hidden profit."
- "Replace your whole tech stack with AI."
- "Set it and forget it."
