# Agent Instructions

These instructions apply to the entire repository.

## Mission

Maintain this repository as a clean, portable operating brain for Kevin Tunis and AI Automation Innovators. Prioritize clarity, reuse, proof, and safe agent collaboration over complexity.

## How to work

1. Read `START-HERE.md` and `README.md` before making broad edits.
2. Check the relevant folder `README.md` before editing files in that folder.
3. Make the smallest useful change.
4. Prefer concise plain language over elaborate frameworks.
5. Keep instructions in one place when possible; link instead of duplicating.

## Do not do these without explicit instruction

- Do not add application code, scripts, build tooling, or automation.
- Do not create unnecessary folders.
- Do not rename files unless the current name causes confusion.
- Do not invent clients, testimonials, dates, revenue, savings, metrics, or outcomes.
- Do not turn draft proof into a case study unless the evidence is present.
- Do not make broad positioning changes without preserving Kevin Tunis, AI Automation Innovators, Workflow Leak Audit, and Central Texas relevance.

## Business positioning guardrails

- Kevin Tunis is the human operator behind AI Automation Innovators.
- AI Automation Innovators helps businesses find and fix workflow leaks using practical AI and automation.
- The Workflow Leak Audit should be described as a practical diagnostic for identifying bottlenecks, manual rework, handoff gaps, missed follow-up, and automation opportunities.
- Central Texas relevance matters, especially Belton, Temple, and nearby service-area context.
- Claims must be supportable from files in this repo or clearly labeled as drafts/placeholders.

## Editing standards

- Use Markdown for knowledge files.
- Keep files scannable with short headings and bullets.
- Mark placeholders clearly with `TODO:`.
- Keep proof separate from positioning and offer copy.
- If you remove or consolidate duplicated guidance, preserve the clearest version.
