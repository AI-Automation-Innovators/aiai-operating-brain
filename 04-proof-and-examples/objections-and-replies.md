# Objections and Replies

## Purpose
General objection-handling language across offers and projects.

## What belongs here
Common objections, honest replies, tone notes, and boundaries.

## What not to include
Manipulative scripts, fake proof, or replies for unconfirmed offers.

## Reply principles
- Answer the concern directly.
- Do not pressure or overpromise.
- Connect the reply to the prospect's workflow, capacity, or follow-up problem.
- Name what is known, what is unknown, and what would be validated in an audit.

## Objection template
```markdown
## Objection
"[Exact objection or paraphrase]"

- Context:
- Audience:
- Offer/project:
- Status: Draft / used / proven

### Short reply
[Concise response]

### Expanded reply
[Longer response]

### Boundary
[What not to claim]

### Follow-up question
[Useful next question]
```

## Common categories
- "We already have tools."
- "We do not need AI."
- "We are too busy to change systems."
- "We tried automation before."
- "What is the ROI?"

## Update rules
Update from real conversations and keep replies direct.
