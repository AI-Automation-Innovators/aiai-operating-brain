# Wins and Receipts

## Purpose
Evidence and proof points that can be referenced safely.

This file exists to make future proof easy to document without fabricating wins, testimonials, metrics, or client details.

## Source of Truth
Only use receipts that include a source, date, permission status, and approved wording. If any part is missing, mark it as `TBD`.

## What Belongs Here
- Confirmed wins.
- Receipts.
- Testimonials.
- Metrics.
- Links.
- Screenshots or source locations.
- Permission notes.
- Approved reusable wording.

## What Not to Include
- Client names without permission.
- Metrics without a source.
- Testimonials without approval.
- Revenue claims that cannot be verified.
- Private or sensitive personal details.

## Current Proof Status
No confirmed wins, receipts, testimonials, metrics, or public proof are documented in this file yet. Treat proof as `TBD` until source, date, permission status, and approved wording are added.

## Receipt Template

```markdown
## Receipt: [Short title]

- Date: [YYYY-MM-DD or TBD]
- What was shipped: [Specific deliverable or TBD]
- Where it was used: [Client/project/channel/internal use or TBD]
- Proof source: [Link, screenshot location, email, doc, CRM note, or TBD]
- Public/private status: [Public / Private / Internal / TBD]
- Permission status: [Approved / Not approved / Needs approval / TBD]
- Reusable wording: [Exact approved wording or TBD]
- Why it matters: [Operational value, customer result, clarity gained, etc.]
- TBD items: [Missing details]

### Evidence
[Link, screenshot location, testimonial, metric, or note]

### Context
[What happened and why it matters]

### Reuse Notes
- Where this can be referenced:
- Required caveats:
- Do not say:
```

## Reuse Rules
- Use exact approved wording when available.
- If permission is private or unknown, do not use the receipt publicly.
- If a metric is not sourced, do not use it.
- If a client name is not approved, anonymize or omit it.
- If a claim could sound like a guarantee, rewrite it as a documented observation or mark it `TBD`.
