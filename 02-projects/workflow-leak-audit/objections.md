# Workflow Leak Audit Objections

## “We already have a CRM.”
That is useful. The audit is not about replacing the CRM first. It checks whether the workflow around the CRM is actually capturing, assigning, following up, and surfacing the right opportunities.

## “We do not need AI.”
That may be true. The audit starts with workflow leaks, not AI for its own sake. If AI is useful, it should support follow-up, visibility, and consistency.

## “We just need more leads.”
More leads can help, but only if the current workflow can handle them. The audit looks for missed opportunity inside the leads and customers the business already has.

## “Our team already follows up.”
Great. The audit looks at consistency: what happens when people are busy, out of office, using different tools, or relying on memory.

## “We are too busy for a big project.”
The audit is intended to identify focused improvements, not create unnecessary complexity. Scope, timeline, and delivery format are still `TBD` until Kevin confirms them.

## “Can you guarantee results?”
No. The audit can identify likely leaks and recommended fixes, but it should not make guaranteed revenue claims.
