# Workflow Leak Audit Ideal Clients

## Purpose
Define who is a good fit for the Workflow Leak Audit and who should not be sold the audit yet.

## Best-Fit Businesses
The Workflow Leak Audit is best for local businesses with repeatable customer workflows and enough activity that missed follow-up creates real cost.

Strong fits include:

- Home services.
- Medical, dental, wellness, or appointment-based practices.
- Professional services.
- Local B2B service providers.
- Businesses with inbound calls, forms, emails, texts, estimates, appointments, or recurring customer communication.

## Good-Fit Traits
- Owner-operated or owner-influenced.
- Practical and open to workflow improvement.
- Willing to share how leads, customers, estimates, jobs, and follow-ups currently move through the business.
- Uses multiple tools or manual steps that create visibility gaps.
- Values clear recommendations over buzzwords.
- Wants to improve consistency before buying more software.

## Signals of Urgent Workflow Leaks
- Leads arrive from multiple sources with no single owner.
- Missed calls or voicemails are not reviewed daily.
- Website forms, texts, emails, and social messages are checked inconsistently.
- Quotes, estimates, or proposals are sent once and not tracked.
- Customers ask for updates because next steps are unclear.
- Staff rely on memory to know what should happen next.
- The owner has to manually chase status across inboxes, texts, spreadsheets, calendars, or CRMs.
- No-shows, cancellations, stale quotes, and completed jobs do not trigger a defined follow-up process.

## Prospect Qualification Questions
- Where do new customer inquiries come from today?
- Who owns first response?
- What happens when someone misses a call or form submission?
- How do you know which estimates or quotes need follow-up?
- What customer opportunities are easiest to lose track of?
- Which parts of the workflow depend on you personally remembering what to do?
- What do you wish you could see at a glance every week?
- Are you looking for a practical audit first, or are you expecting a full software build right away?

## Not a Good Fit
- Businesses looking only for generic AI content or chatbots.
- Teams unwilling to document their current process.
- Prospects demanding guaranteed revenue outcomes.
- Companies that want a large custom software project before agreeing on workflow basics.
- Businesses that will not allow enough process visibility to identify leaks.
- Prospects expecting customer-facing automation without human approval rules.
- Teams that are not ready to review findings or make basic owner decisions.

## Not Ready Yet Signals
- No clear owner for the audit conversation.
- The prospect cannot describe how leads or customers currently move through the business.
- The prospect wants AI hype but not operational review.
- The prospect refuses to mark unknowns as `TBD`.
- The prospect expects guarantees, invented benchmarks, or unsupported revenue projections.
