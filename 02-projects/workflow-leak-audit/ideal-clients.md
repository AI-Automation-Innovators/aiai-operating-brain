# Ideal Clients

## Primary Client Types
- Local service businesses
- Home service companies
- Real estate teams
- Local professional services
- Small businesses in Belton, Temple, Salado, Killeen, Harker Heights, and Central Texas

## Strong Fit Signals
- They receive calls, website inquiries, emails, referrals, or social messages.
- They send quotes, estimates, proposals, or service recommendations.
- Follow-up depends on staff remembering what to do.
- The owner often knows what is happening, but the system does not.
- The CRM exists but is not consistently updated.
- Leads or quotes are not reviewed weekly.
- Past customers are not being reactivated.
- They suspect opportunities are being missed but cannot see where.

## Common Pain Points
- Missed calls
- Slow response times
- Forgotten estimates
- Untracked lead sources
- No clear owner for follow-up
- No simple weekly open-opportunity report
- Busy staff and inconsistent admin work

## Buying Trigger
The owner or operator realizes that more revenue may be available from existing leads, quotes, customers, and inquiries before spending more money on marketing.

## Messaging Angle
Focus on tightening follow-up, quote recovery, and missed-opportunity workflows. Keep AI in the background as the operating layer that can help the fix run consistently.
