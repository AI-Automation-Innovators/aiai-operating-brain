# Workflow Leak Audit Intake Questionnaire

## Purpose
Draft intake questions for understanding a local business workflow before a Workflow Leak Audit. Keep answers factual and mark unknowns as `TBD`.

## Business context
1. What type of business is this?
2. What service area or location does it serve?
3. Who owns the customer workflow day to day?
4. Which roles are involved in leads, follow-up, handoffs, appointments, jobs, or customer recovery?

## Lead capture
1. Where do new inquiries arrive?
2. Are all lead sources visible in one place?
3. Who owns the first response?
4. How quickly does first response usually happen?
5. What happens when the owner or staff are busy?

## Follow-up
1. What happens after the first reply?
2. Are quotes, estimates, proposals, or appointments tracked?
3. Are second and third touches scheduled or remembered manually?
4. Where do stale opportunities get reviewed?
5. What follow-up currently falls through the cracks?

## Handoffs
1. Where does responsibility move from one person to another?
2. What information gets retyped, copied, or manually transferred?
3. Where do misunderstandings or delays happen?
4. How does the next owner know what to do?

## Customer recovery
1. Are no-shows, cancellations, stale quotes, or inactive customers reviewed?
2. Is there a process for reactivation?
3. Are completed jobs followed by review requests, referrals, or repeat-work prompts?
4. Which customer opportunities are most often forgotten after the job or appointment?

## Visibility
1. Can the owner quickly see what is stuck?
2. Which workflows are hidden in email, text, spreadsheets, calendars, CRMs, or memory?
3. What dashboard, report, or list would make the workflow easier to manage?
4. What does the owner wish they could see every week?

## Tools
1. Which CRM, inbox, calendar, phone, text, spreadsheet, or project tools are currently used?
2. Which tools are trusted?
3. Which tools are ignored, duplicated, or worked around?
4. Which tools should not be changed without strong reason?

## Success criteria
1. What would make this audit useful?
2. Which workflow leak would be most valuable to fix first?
3. What should not be changed?
4. What decisions does Kevin need to make after reviewing the audit?
