# Workflow Leak Audit Outreach Examples

## Purpose
Reusable outreach and follow-up language for the Workflow Leak Audit.

## Positioning Note
Outreach should lead with missed opportunity and workflow clarity, not AI novelty.

Keep wording professional, local, and practical. Do not imply guaranteed revenue recovery, confirmed proof, pricing, or timeline unless those details are approved elsewhere in the repository.

## Short Email Draft
Subject: Quick question about follow-up gaps

Hi [Name],

A lot of local businesses are not losing money because they need more leads. They are losing it because good inquiries, estimates, and customer follow-ups get buried across inboxes, texts, calendars, and CRMs.

I run a Workflow Leak Audit that maps where those opportunities are slipping through and ranks the easiest fixes.

Would it be worth a quick conversation to see if there are any obvious leaks in your current follow-up process?

Kevin Tunis
AI Automation Innovators

## LinkedIn Message Draft
Hi [Name] — I help local businesses find workflow leaks: missed follow-ups, stuck estimates, slow handoffs, and customer opportunities buried in the day-to-day. If useful, I can share what a simple Workflow Leak Audit looks for.

## Plain-English Discovery Flow

### Opening
- “I am not trying to sell you a big AI build before understanding the workflow.”
- “The audit is about finding where opportunities get delayed, dropped, or hidden.”
- “If there is not a real leak, I do not want to force one.”

### Discovery Questions
- “Where do most new inquiries come from?”
- “What happens when someone misses a call, form, text, or email?”
- “How do you know which estimates need follow-up?”
- “Where do handoffs get messy between people, tools, or stages?”
- “What does the owner have to remember manually?”
- “What opportunities are most likely to go stale?”
- “If you could see one workflow list every week, what would it show?”

### Qualification Questions
- “Would a ranked list of workflow leaks be useful even before talking about tools?”
- “Are you open to reviewing the current process honestly?”
- “Do you want an audit first, or are you expecting implementation right away?”
- “Who would need to approve any follow-up process changes?”

### Close
- “Based on what you shared, I can either recommend a Workflow Leak Audit or tell you it is probably not the right next step.”
- “If we do the audit, the goal is to identify the highest-priority leaks and separate quick wins from anything that needs separate approval.”

- [Leak area 3]

The audit would not assume a full automation build. Any implementation would need separate approval.

- [Finding 2]
- [Finding 3]

### 3. No-Response Follow-Up

Hi [Name],

Just checking back on the Workflow Leak Audit conversation.

If now is not the right time, no problem. I can park it. If follow-up gaps, stale estimates, missed calls, or handoff issues are still on your radar, we can use the audit to identify what is actually worth fixing first.

Should I keep this open or close the loop for now?

Kevin Tunis
AI Automation Innovators

### 4. Final Check-In
Subject: Closing the loop

Hi [Name],

I am going to close the loop for now.

If workflow leaks become a priority later, the simplest place to restart is with one question: where are good customer opportunities getting delayed, dropped, or hidden today?

## Avoid
- “AI will transform your business.”
- “Guaranteed revenue recovery.”
- “You are leaving millions on the table.”
- Any claim that implies proof not already documented.
- Any promise that implementation is included by default.
