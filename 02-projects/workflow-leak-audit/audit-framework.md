# Workflow Leak Audit Framework

Use this framework to rank workflow leaks by revenue impact, ease of implementation, and urgency.

## 1. Intake and Discovery
### What to Look For
How inquiries, calls, emails, forms, referrals, and tasks enter the business.

### Why It Matters
Leaks often start before a lead is ever tracked.

### Example Questions
- Where do new inquiries come from?
- Who sees them first?
- What happens if that person is busy?
- Which steps depend on memory?

### Possible Automation Fix
Central intake log, missed-inquiry notification, or simple routing workflow.

## 2. Lead Source Review
### What to Look For
Whether lead sources are known, tracked, and connected to booked conversations.

### Why It Matters
Local visibility does not help if it does not turn into follow-up.

### Example Questions
- Do you know which sources create real conversations?
- Are calls, website inquiries, and referrals tracked in one place?
- Which source is easiest to lose track of?

### Possible Automation Fix
Lead source tagging, weekly source report, or intake form standardization.

## 3. Follow-Up Review
### What to Look For
Speed, consistency, ownership, and visibility of follow-up.

### Why It Matters
Slow or inconsistent follow-up turns active interest into missed opportunity.

### Example Questions
- How quickly do new inquiries receive a response?
- Who owns the next follow-up?
- How do you know nothing was forgotten?

### Possible Automation Fix
Follow-up reminders, task creation, response templates, or follow-up recovery workflow.

## 4. Quote and Estimate Review
### What to Look For
How quotes are sent, tracked, followed up, and closed.

### Why It Matters
Unfollowed quotes may represent revenue already earned but not recovered.

### Example Questions
- How many open quotes are currently waiting?
- When does follow-up happen after an estimate?
- Who reviews old quotes weekly?

### Possible Automation Fix
Open quote tracker, quote follow-up sequence, or weekly recovery list.

## 5. CRM or Tracking Review
### What to Look For
Whether the CRM or tracking sheet reflects reality.

### Why It Matters
A system that is not updated cannot protect revenue.

### Example Questions
- What information is missing most often?
- Who updates the system?
- What happens when the system is not updated?

### Possible Automation Fix
CRM update prompts, simplified fields, automated task creation, or weekly exception report.

## 6. Dormant Customer Review
### What to Look For
Past customers who have not been contacted, rebooked, or reactivated.

### Why It Matters
Dormant customers may be the fastest path to recovered revenue.

### Example Questions
- When was the last customer reactivation campaign?
- Which customers should hear from you again?
- Do you have a repeat service or check-in process?

### Possible Automation Fix
Dormant customer list, reactivation messages, seasonal reminders, or customer check-in workflow.

## 7. Manual Admin Review
### What to Look For
Repeatable tasks handled manually, inconsistently, or only by the owner.

### Why It Matters
Manual admin gaps create delays and keep the owner in the middle of routine work.

### Example Questions
- Which tasks are repeated every week?
- Which tasks stop when someone is busy?
- What does only the owner know how to do?

### Possible Automation Fix
Task templates, status dashboards, handoff checklists, or semi-automated admin workflows.

## 8. Revenue Recovery Opportunities
### What to Look For
Open estimates, missed inquiries, dormant customers, and overdue follow-ups.

### Why It Matters
The easiest win is often revenue already inside the business.

### Example Questions
- What opportunities are open right now?
- Which ones are most likely to close with follow-up?
- What would a weekly recovery process need to show?

### Possible Automation Fix
Ranked recovery list, weekly report, or follow-up recovery workflow.

## 9. Automation Readiness
### What to Look For
Workflow clarity, data quality, staff adoption risk, and tool simplicity.

### Why It Matters
Automation works best after the business understands the workflow it wants to improve.

### Example Questions
- Is the current process written down?
- Is the data reliable enough to trigger follow-up?
- What would the team actually use?

### Possible Automation Fix
Start with a small semi-automated workflow before adding a larger system.

## 10. Recommended Next Step
### What to Look For
The highest-value leak that is practical to fix first.

### Why It Matters
The audit should lead to action, not a long list of ideas.

### Example Questions
- Which leak is costing the most?
- Which fix is easiest to implement?
- What should be reviewed weekly?

### Possible Automation Fix
A focused implementation proposal for the first recovery workflow.
