# Workflow Leak Audit Instructions

## Purpose
The Workflow Leak Audit is the primary local business offer for AI Automation Innovators.

It helps local businesses find missed opportunities caused by manual, inconsistent, untracked, or memory-dependent workflows.

## Positioning
- Lead with business outcomes, not AI tools.
- Use “Workflow Leak Audit,” not “free AI audit.”
- Use “missed opportunity” when describing workflow gaps.
- Use “follow-up recovery” instead of “chatbot.”
- Use “revenue already earned but not recovered” when appropriate.
- Position AI as the operating layer behind the fix, not the headline.
- Find workflow gaps before recommending tools.

## Primary Audience
- Local service businesses
- Home service companies
- Real estate teams
- Local professional services
- Small businesses in Belton, Temple, Salado, Killeen, Harker Heights, and Central Texas
- Businesses with leads, customers, quotes, inquiries, or repeatable admin tasks but inconsistent follow-up

## Core Leaks to Watch For
- Missed calls or website inquiries
- Slow email response
- Forgotten quotes or estimates
- No follow-up after estimates
- Manual intake gaps
- Untracked lead sources
- CRM not updated
- Dormant customers with no reactivation process
- No weekly visibility into open opportunities
- Staff too busy to follow up consistently
- Owner carrying too much operational memory
- Local visibility not turning into booked conversations

## Audit Outcome
By the end of the audit, the business should understand:
1. Where opportunities are leaking
2. What the leak may be costing them
3. Which leaks are easiest to fix first
4. Which workflow could be automated or semi-automated
5. What the next practical implementation step should be

## Output Style
Keep recommendations practical, ranked, and tied to revenue recovery, follow-up gaps, missed opportunities, and operational visibility.
